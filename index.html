FINANCIAL VIABILITY ANALYZER
// objective financial analysis

Does the math back your gut?

Enter raw numbers from Yahoo Finance. The tool computes 18 ratios, scores each against sector-adjusted benchmarks, and tells you whether the financials support an investment thesis.

NOT financial advice. Benchmarks are generalised — always compare to direct sector peers. Point-in-time snapshot only.
SECTOR Benchmarks adjust to your selected sector.
1
Income Statement
Revenue · Margins · Earnings
▼
iYahoo Finance → ticker → Financials → Income Statement → Annual. Use consistent units throughout ($M or $B).
Revenue — Year 1 (most recent)
Total sales. The topmost line of the income statement. Used for all margin and growth calculations.
Income Statement → Total Revenue → most recent year
Same unit ($M or $B) for all fields
Revenue — Year 2 (prior year)
One year before Year 1. Used to compute year-over-year revenue growth rate.
Income Statement → Total Revenue → prior year
Revenue — Year 3 (two years prior)
Two years before Year 1. Used to compute 2-year CAGR, a smoother growth measure.
Income Statement → Total Revenue → two years prior
Gross Profit
Revenue minus cost of goods sold. Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Revenue. The most fundamental margin — reveals pricing power and product economics. High gross margin means room to invest in growth and absorb cost increases.
Income Statement → Gross Profit
Operating Income (EBIT)
Profit from core operations after all operating costs but before interest and taxes. Operating Margin = EBIT / Revenue. Shows whether the actual business model is profitable, independent of financing.
Income Statement → Operating Income
Net Income
Bottom-line profit after all expenses, interest, and taxes. Net Margin = Net Income / Revenue. Compare to Free Cash Flow — if net income is persistently higher, earnings quality may be suspect.
Income Statement → Net Income (bottom of statement)
EBITDA
Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortisation. Used to compute the Net Debt/EBITDA leverage ratio. Listed directly in Yahoo's income statement.
Income Statement → EBITDA (scroll down)
Interest Expense
Annual cost of servicing debt. Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expense. Below 2x means most operating profit goes to debt service. Enter as a positive number even though it appears negative on the statement.
Income Statement → Interest Expense (enter as positive)
2
Balance Sheet
Cash · Debt · Equity · Liquidity
▼
iYahoo Finance → Financials → Balance Sheet → Annual. Use the most recent year. Same units as income statement.
Cash & Short-Term Investments
All cash plus near-cash investments. Subtracted from Total Debt to compute Net Debt. A large cash balance is a fortress signal — the company can weather downturns without needing external capital.
Balance Sheet → Cash And Cash Equivalents (or Cash And Short Term Investments)
Total Debt
All interest-bearing borrowings — short-term and long-term combined. Net Debt = Total Debt minus Cash. Net Debt/EBITDA above 3.5x is elevated in most sectors.
Balance Sheet → Total Debt
Total Assets
Everything the company owns. Used to compute Return on Assets (ROA = Net Income / Total Assets) — how efficiently the company turns its asset base into profit.
Balance Sheet → Total Assets
Total Shareholders' Equity
Assets minus all liabilities. Used for ROE and Debt/Equity ratio. Companies with heavy buybacks or past losses can show negative equity — enter with a negative sign if so.
Balance Sheet → Total Stockholders' Equity
Total Current Assets
Assets convertible to cash within 12 months. Numerator of the Current Ratio (Current Assets / Current Liabilities).
Balance Sheet → Total Current Assets
Total Current Liabilities
Obligations due within 12 months. Current Ratio below 1.0 means the company cannot cover near-term obligations from existing current assets alone — a liquidity warning.
Balance Sheet → Total Current Liabilities
3
Cash Flow Statement
Operating CF · CapEx · Free Cash Flow
▼
iYahoo Finance → Financials → Cash Flow → Annual. Cash flow is the hardest financial statement to manipulate — it is the most reliable measure of business health.
Operating Cash Flow
Cash generated from running the business. The purest measure of operational health. Consistently lower than Net Income is a major red flag — earnings may be inflated through accounting choices.
Cash Flow → Operating Cash Flow (top section)
Capital Expenditure (CapEx)
Cash spent maintaining or expanding physical assets. Free Cash Flow = Operating Cash Flow minus CapEx. CapEx appears negative on Yahoo — enter it here as a positive number.
Cash Flow → Capital Expenditure (enter as positive)
4
Valuation Multiples
P/E · EV/EBITDA · P/S · P/B
▼
iYahoo Finance → search ticker → Statistics tab → Valuation Measures. Pre-computed. Always compare to direct industry peers, not just these benchmarks.
Trailing P/E Ratio
Price divided by last 12 months of actual earnings per share. High P/E means high growth expectations baked in — any miss triggers sharp selloffs. N/A for loss-making companies.
Statistics → Valuation Measures → Trailing P/E
Forward P/E Ratio
Price divided by next 12 months' estimated earnings. Lower than trailing means analysts expect earnings growth. Higher than trailing means earnings are expected to shrink.
Statistics → Valuation Measures → Forward P/E
EV / EBITDA
Enterprise Value divided by EBITDA. More complete than P/E because it accounts for debt load. Widely used to compare companies with different capital structures.
Statistics → Valuation Measures → Enterprise Value/EBITDA
Price / Sales (P/S)
Market cap divided by annual revenue. Useful for pre-profit companies. A high P/S is only justified by high margins and strong growth — context is everything here.
Statistics → Valuation Measures → Price/Sales (ttm)
Price / Book (P/B)
Price divided by book value per share. Below 1.0 means the market values the company below its net assets. Banks are traditionally evaluated heavily on P/B.
Statistics → Valuation Measures → Price/Book (mrq)
5
Returns & Efficiency
ROE · ROA
▼
iYahoo Finance → Statistics → Management Effectiveness. Pre-computed. Leave blank to auto-compute from your inputs above.
Return on Equity (ROE)
Net Income / Shareholders' Equity as a percentage. Very high ROE driven by heavy debt (low equity) can be misleading — always check the leverage situation alongside ROE.
Statistics → Management Effectiveness → Return on Equity (ttm)
Enter as % — leave blank to auto-compute
Return on Assets (ROA)
Net Income / Total Assets as a percentage. Asset-heavy industries naturally score lower than asset-light businesses — always compare within sector.
Statistics → Management Effectiveness → Return on Assets (ttm)
Enter as % — leave blank to auto-compute
All fields are optional. More fields = more accurate result. Minimum for a meaningful verdict: Revenue (3 years), Gross Profit, Operating Income, Net Income, EBITDA, Cash, Total Debt, Operating Cash Flow, CapEx.
